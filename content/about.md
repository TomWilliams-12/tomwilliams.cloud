## From IT support to cloud engineering

My journey into tech started in 2015 at **Events Photo Team**, where I cut my teeth in IT support — keeping systems running during live events and learning the fundamentals of troubleshooting under pressure. It was hands-on, fast-paced, and gave me a solid foundation in how technology serves real users.

From there I moved to **Installing IT** as a **Senior IT Engineer**, broadening my experience across a range of client environments and technologies. I then spent four years at **Elan Homes** as an **IT Administrator**, where I was responsible for managing internal infrastructure end-to-end: Active Directory, group policy, networking, on-prem servers, and user support across the business. That role taught me what it means to own infrastructure — not just build it, but keep it running, secure, and evolving.

## The move to cloud

In 2021, I joined **Edmentum** as a **Cloud Engineer** and made the leap into AWS full-time. It was exactly the challenge I was looking for. Over the past four-plus years I've been promoted through three levels:

- **Cloud Engineer** (2021–2023)
- **Cloud Engineer II** (2023–2025)
- **Senior Cloud Engineer** (2025–present)

## What I do day-to-day

I build and maintain AWS infrastructure across a multi-account organisation. The toolbox is wide — **AWS Organizations**, **Landing Zones**, **Account Factory for Terraform (AFT)**, **Terraform**, **CloudFormation**, **Lambda**, **EventBridge**, **Systems Manager**, and more. I work on CI/CD pipelines with **Azure DevOps** and **GitHub Actions**, implement security tooling with **Security Hub**, **GuardDuty**, **Inspector**, and **AWS Config**, and lead migrations of on-prem and legacy systems into AWS.

Day-to-day, that means everything from writing Terraform modules and automating compliance remediation to architecting new account structures and mentoring team members on cloud best practices.
